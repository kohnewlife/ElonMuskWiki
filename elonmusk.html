Elon Musk

The real life Iron Man

Musk is the founder, CEO, and CTO of SpaceX; a co-founder, a Series A investor, CEO, and product architect of Tesla Inc. He was previously co-founder and chairman of SolarCity;

Here's a time line of Elon Musk's life:

  • 1971 - Born in Pretoria, Transvaal, South Africa.
  • 1983 - Taught himself computer programming at the age of 12, sold the code for a BASIC-based video game he created called Blastar for approximately $500.
  • 1989 - Graduated from Pretoria Boys High School and moved to Canada
  • 1992 - After spending two years at Queen's University, Musk transferred to the University of Pennsylvania
  • 1995 - Musk and his brother, Kimbal, started Zip2, a web software company, with US$28,000 of their father's money.
  • 1997 - Received a Bachelor of Science degree in physics from its College of Arts and Sciences, and a Bachelor of Science degree in economics
  • 1999 - Musk received US$22 million for his 7% share from selling Zip2 to Compaq
  • 1999 - Right afer selling Zip2, Musk co-founded X.com, an online financial services and e-mail payment company which was then renamed Paypal in 2001.
  • 2002 - With US$100 million of his early fortune,[65] Musk founded Space Exploration Technologies, or SpaceX, in May 2002.
  • 2002 - PayPal was acquired by eBay for US$1.5 billion in stock, of which Musk received US$165 million.
  • 2006 - SpaceX was awarded a contract from NASA to continue the development and test of the SpaceX Falcon 9 launch vehicle and Dragon spacecraft in order to transport cargo to the International Space Station
  • 2006 - Musk provided the initial concept and financial capital for SolarCity, which was then co-founded by his cousins Lyndon and Peter Rive.
  • 2008 - Musk assumed leadership of Tesla Motors company as CEO and product architect, positions he still holds today.
  • 2008 - SpaceX received a US$1.6 billion NASA Commercial Resupply Services program contract for 12 flights of its Falcon 9 rocket and Dragon spacecraft to the Space Station, replacing the US Space Shuttle after it retired in 2011.
  • 2012 - Tesla began delivery of its four-door Model S sedan.
  • 2014 - Musk committed to building in Buffalo, New York, a SolarCity advanced production facility that would triple the size of the largest solar plant in the United States. Musk stated the plant will be "one of the single largest solar panel production plants in the world", and it will be followed by one or more even bigger facilities in subsequent years.
  • 2016 - Musk's car company, Tesla, formally submitted an offer to acquire SolarCity.
  • 2017 - On January 21, Musk tweeted "Exciting progress on the tunnel front. Plan to start digging in a month or so."[127] The first tunnel will start on the SpaceX campus,[128] and will probably go to a nearby parking garage. As of January 26, 2017, discussions with regulatory bodies have begun,[129] but no requests for permits to dig in the Los Angeles area have yet been filed with the California Department of Transportation.
